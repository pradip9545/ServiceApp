Shiva Gadag

Dr. Shiva Gadag earned his MSc (Engg) and PhD in Mechanical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Science, a premier research institute for advanced study and research He obtained Ph.D. in 1992 for a thesis on Laser Processing of Ductile iron, the first thesis in the area of high power laser materials processing from the faculty of engineering of INDIAN INSTITUTE OF SCIENCE. In 1993 he was invited by KFA Julich on behalf of the German Government to work as a Visiting Scientist with Professor Barry Mordike at the TU Clausthal under the Indo-German bilateral research collaboration on Laser Materials Processing. He presented his research finding on laser alloying and cladding of copper at the NATO Advanced Study Institute held in Sesimbra, Portugal in 1994. After his return from Germany, worked as the Indian Institute of Science Research Associate from 1995-97.

hen he has worked as a Project Scientist at the University of California, San Diego from 1997-00, Research Associate in UC Boulder (2001-03), Old Dominion University (2004-05) Purdue University (2005-06) and University of Central Florida in Orlando (2012-13) and served as a Research Engineer TATA Consultancy Services USA (2014-2015) He has worked in various areas of the laser materials processing - laser based melting and transformation hardening, solid state nitriding, Nano sintering, Micro alloying, cladding, thin film deposition and laser Synthesis of nanostructures. He has used high power lasers - CO2 CW, Direct Diode Laser-HPDL, Fiber coupled Diode Laser, Nd:YAG and Nd:YVO4 repetitively pulsed and Q-switched diode pumped solid state DPSS lasers for his research work.

Recently he has worked as research assistant at Southern Methodist University from 2008-2011, in the area of laser micromachining of Si and its Transparent Dielectrics - glass and quartz materials with multi-wavelengths (1064, 532,355 and 266nm), nanoshort (11 ns), repetitive frequency (16 kHz) Q-switched Spectra Physics lasers. He earned another PhD on Laser micromachining of silicon and glass with short pulsed lasers from Southern Methodist University. He has invented the mechanism of micromachining and formulated theory of laser ablation of single crystal Silicon using ultrashort and ultrafast IR lasers pulse and numerically simulated and experimentally proved the ablation of silicon.
